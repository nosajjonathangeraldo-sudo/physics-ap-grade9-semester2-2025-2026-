Speed, Distance & Time

Speed, distance, and time are three quantities that describe how an object moves. Understanding their relationship allows you to calculate any one quantity if the other two are known.

Distance

Distance is the total path traveled by an object, regardless of direction.

Total Distance = Sum of all paths traveled

Example 1: A person walks 5 m forward, then 5 m back. Total distance = 5 + 5 = 10 m.

Example 2: A runner completes two laps around a 400 m track. Total distance = 400 × 2 = 800 m.

Time

Time is how long it takes to cover a certain distance. Measured in seconds (s), minutes (min), or hours (h).

Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Example: A cyclist travels 20 km at 5 km/h. Time = 20 ÷ 5 = 4 hours.

Speed

Speed tells how fast an object moves.

Speed = Distance ÷ Time

Example 1: Car travels 100 m in 5 s → 100 ÷ 5 = 20 m/s.

Example 2: Train covers 150 km in 3 hours → 150 ÷ 3 = 50 km/h.

Rearranging Formulas

Distance = Speed × Time
Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Example: Runner moves at 4 m/s for 15 s → Distance = 4 × 15 = 60 m.

Acceleration

Acceleration measures how quickly an object's velocity changes over time. Velocity includes speed and direction, so acceleration occurs when an object speeds up, slows down, or changes direction.

  • Speeding up → positive acceleration
  • Slowing down → negative acceleration (deceleration)
  • Constant speed → zero acceleration
a = (v − u) ÷ t

Where: v = final velocity, u = initial velocity, t = time taken

Example 1: Car speeding up

Car accelerates from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 5 s.

Step 1: Identify values → u = 10, v = 30, t = 5

Step 2: a = (v − u) ÷ t = (30 − 10) ÷ 5

Step 3: a = 20 ÷ 5 = 4 m/s² → The car's speed increases by 4 m/s every second.

Example 2: Bicycle slowing down

Bicycle slows from 12 m/s to 4 m/s in 2 s.

a = (4 − 12) ÷ 2 = -8 ÷ 2 = -4 m/s² → Negative acceleration means deceleration.

Example 3: Rocket launch

Rocket goes from 0 to 100 m/s in 10 s → a = (100 − 0) ÷ 10 = 10 m/s².

This means the rocket’s speed increases by 10 m/s each second.

Example 4: Multiple-step problem

A car accelerates from 5 m/s to 25 m/s in 4 s, then continues for another 2 s at constant speed. Find acceleration in the first phase and total distance covered.

Step 1: Acceleration = (25 − 5) ÷ 4 = 20 ÷ 4 = 5 m/s²

Step 2: Distance during acceleration = u×t + 0.5×a×t² = 5×4 + 0.5×5×16 = 20 + 40 = 60 m

Step 3: Distance at constant speed = speed × time = 25 × 2 = 50 m

Total distance = 60 + 50 = 110 m
